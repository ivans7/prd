# Market And BizDev

Use this skill when the user needs an external market view or a commercial growth strategy.

For Alloy/ITSM work, ground the analysis in the actual product model and category realities:
- ITSM / service desk / asset management / discovery / workflow automation positioning
- Express vs Enterprise packaging and capability boundaries
- object model implications for positioning and packaging, not just UI-level features
- claims about automation should map to actual workflow surfaces such as actions, triggers, templates, notifications, and policies

## Workflow

1. Clarify the commercial question:
   - competitor comparison
   - market map
   - positioning or differentiation
   - pricing and packaging
   - partnership or channel motion
2. Build the right comparison set:
   - direct competitors
   - adjacent alternatives
   - substitutes and manual workflows
3. Choose the few dimensions that matter:
   - ICP and buyer
   - positioning
   - workflow depth and extensibility
   - pricing and packaging
   - implementation or admin complexity
   - partner/channel fit
4. End with implications:
   - where parity is enough
   - where differentiation is required
   - what GTM or partnership motion is most credible next

## Output Rules

- Treat public marketing claims as claims unless backed by product evidence.
- Compare competitors on workflow and operating model, not only feature checklists.
- When discussing Express vs Enterprise or editioning, make the boundary explicit.
- Prefer decision-oriented matrices and recommended actions over exhaustive market notes.
